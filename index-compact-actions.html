Your entries

Sleep efficiency means the percentage of your time in bed that you were actually asleep. In CBT-I, a rough target is usually about 85% or higher. Very roughly: if it stays at 85%+ for a week, the sleep window may be widened; if it stays low, the routine often stays the same or gets tightened.
Time asleep ÷ time in bed × 100
6 hours asleep in 7 hours in bed = 86%

When to widen the window

85% or higherUsually widen by 15 minutes and hold that new window for about a week.
80% to 85%Usually keep the same window for another week.
Below 80%Often tighten by 15 minutes, unless you are already at the minimum allowed window.
Maximum targetKeep widening gradually until sleep feels sufficient for daytime functioning. In practice, many adults finish around 7 to 8 hours in bed, sometimes up to the usual adult recommendation of 7 to 9 hours if sleep stays solid.
Minimum safety floorTime in bed should generally not be reduced below about 5 to 5.5 hours without clinical supervision.

Stimulus control explained

Stimulus control means teaching your brain that bed is for sleep, not for being awake, frustrated, scrolling, or clock-watching.
Go to bed only when sleepyNot just because the clock says bedtime.
Use bed only for sleep and sexNo TV, work, doomscrolling, or long awake periods in bed.
If awake 15-20 minutes, get out of bedGo somewhere dim and calm until you feel sleepy again.
Return only when sleepyIf you go back too early and stay awake, repeat the process.
Keep the same wake timeThe fixed 6:00 am wake time strengthens the sleep rhythm.
Avoid napsNaps reduce sleep drive and weaken the reset.

CBT-I course timeline

Most CBT-I plans run for about 6 to 8 weeks. Early weeks feel stricter because the main job is building consistency and stronger sleep drive.
Weeks 1-2Lock in your 6:00 am wake time, keep the bedtime window, stop napping, and fill in the diary every morning.
Weeks 3-4Sleep usually starts to feel more consolidated. Stick with stimulus control: if you are awake too long, get out of bed and return only when sleepy.
Weeks 5-6If your sleep efficiency is improving, the sleep window can sometimes be widened gradually in small steps.
Weeks 7-8Focus on keeping gains steady, spotting setbacks early, and keeping the same rules when you have a rough night.

How it develops

At the startYou may feel more tired for a short period because the routine is tighter and your body is rebuilding sleep drive.
ThenThe main signs of progress are often less time awake in bed, fewer long wake-ups, and better sleep efficiency before total sleep time increases.
LaterOnce sleep is steadier, bedtime can be adjusted more flexibly, but the fixed wake time normally stays in place.
After the courseKeep using the core rules as maintenance. If sleep slips, go back to the diary and the stricter routine for a few days.
